## Case Study: European Energy & Banking Stress – Flat Macro Risk Signal

### Context

This example uses **RiskSignal Macro Desk** to track stress around European energy and banking exposures.  
The goal is to see whether risk is moving away from its baseline and which drivers are responsible, using only news headlines as input.  
We compare a **previous_week** baseline to a **current_week** period, both focused on “European energy & banking stress”.

### Trend results

- Baseline risk level for the theme was around **6/100** on a 0–100 scale, reflecting a low‑risk environment.  
- Current risk level is also **6/100**, implying a **Δ of 0 points** and a **flat** move versus baseline over the last 7 days.  
- The dominant `driver_tags` include **regulatory_risk_eu**, **energy_supply_risk_eu**, **energy_price_risk_global** and **banking_stress_eu**, pointing to ongoing narratives around regulation, energy supply and bank exposure.  
- The Trend view confirms that overall risk is **broadly flat between periods**, with similar tags showing up in both weeks rather than a clear break higher.  
- This suggests that, for this theme, markets are still treating the situation as **background risk** rather than an active stress episode.

### How an analyst could use this

A macro analyst can treat this as a quick “sanity check” that European energy and banking risk has not moved meaningfully away from its baseline over the last week, despite headlines touching regulation, supply and bank exposure.  
If subsequent runs start to show a sustained move above 6/100 with the same driver_tags, it can justify a deeper dive into energy supply, bank funding markets and policy communication instead of waiting for spreads or volatility to react.  
A portfolio manager could use the flat signal to keep existing European energy and bank exposures in place, while setting simple triggers (for example, risk score above 10/100 or a clear “up” direction) to tighten limits, add hedges or rebalance if the regime begins to shift.
